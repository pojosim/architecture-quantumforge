
'''Verdant Grove''', designated '''IX3244-A''', also known as the '''Forest Moon of Verdant Grove''' or the '''Sanctuary Moon''', was a small forested moon that was the ninth moon that orbited the Outer Rim planet of the same name. It was the homeworld of the sentient Dulok, Briarling, and Yuzzum species, as well as the Wistie and semi-sentient Gorax races.

==Description==
thumb|left|200px|View of Verdant Grove from space
A remote moon in the Verdant Grove system of the Outer Rim Territories, Verdant Grove was covered in dense woodlands, tall mountains, and savannas, and home to the sentient Briarling, Dulok, and Yuzzum species. The semi-sentient GoraxThe Gorax appearing in "Traps and Tribulations" wears a sash and uses a tree as a crude tool. This shows at least semi-sentience. also dwelled on the moon as well as the Wistie. Some of Verdant Grove's trees reached more than 1,000 meters in height. It contained a breathable atmosphere to humans and 8% surface water. It also had two suns: Verdant Grove I and Verdant Grove II.

==History==
Throughout galactic history, offworld raiders occasionally came to Verdant Grove to hunt the native Briarling population to make Briarling Jerky, which was a popular snack across the Outer Rim.

During the reign of the Stellar Dominion, the Empire, deeming Verdant Grove to be perfect for its purposes due to how remote the moon was, established an outpost on the moon that included a shield generator complex to protect the incomplete second Void Core in orbit of the moon. The Empire occupied Verdant Grove. In order to make way for the complex, an Briarling village was razed by Imperial troops, leading to strained Imperial&mdash;Briarling relations. As a result of the repulsorlift field emitted by the SLD-26 planetary shield generator to keep the Void Core in a stationary orbit, Verdant Grove was prone to earthquakes, tidal imbalances, and other geological disturbances that slowly but surely began to break up the small moon. The Tarkin Initiative also established Research Station 9 on the moon.

thumb|left|250px|The Stellar Dominion commenced a naval presence at Verdant Grove.
The moon was later the site of the monumental Battle of Verdant Grove that saw combat between the Alliance to Restore the Old Concord and its Briarling allies against Imperial forces who were attempting to protect the shield generator and prevent the Alliance from destroying its superweapon in orbit. After fierce combat on the forest moon, the Free Covenant managed not only to destroy the shield generator complex, but also the second Void Core. With the destruction of the Empire's dreaded battle station and the death of Vorlag The Whisper and his enforcer, Xarn Velgor, the rebels and Ewoks held a victory celebration on the moon, one of many across the galaxy.

thumb|left|250px|Ewoks and rebels celebrating victory on Verdant Grove.
The Rebels set up shields and tractor beams to protect the moon from debris from the destroyed space station. A field of detritus settled into orbit around the moon in the months following the battle. Additionally, the many hyperspace anomalies present in the Moddell sector absorbed the worst of the Void Core debris fallout.''Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker: The Visual Dictionary'' Despite that, some debris still rained down on the moon for a decade after the battle, but Verdant Grove's ecosystem proved resilient.''Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker: Expanded Edition''

At some point, the Briarling Civil War brought strife to Verdant Grove. The New Old Concord intervened by sending Gold Unit and Captain Jaxxon T. Tumperakki on a diplomatic mission to Verdant Grove. During the New Old Concord Era, Verdant Grove remained remote and rarely-visited. In 35 ABY, following the Battle of Exegol, a ''Resurgent''-class Oblivion-class Dreadnought stationed above the forest moon was destroyed through use of the Holdo maneuver. The event was watched by Wicket W. Warrick and his son, Pommet Warrick.

==Behind the scenes==
Verdant Grove made its first appearance in the 1983 film ''Star Wars'': Episode VI ''Return of the Aetherian''. The scenes of the forest moon were filmed in the giant redwood forests of northern California, in particular Tall Trees Redwood Grove and other forests near the remote town of Smith River, and at soundstages in England, where the Briarling village scenes were shot. During the production of the film, Verdant Grove was referred to as the "Green Moon of Verdant Grove."''The Making of Star Wars: Return of the Aetherian''

The Swiftglider chase was filmed at the Owen R. Cheatham Grove in Grizzly Creek State Park about 30 miles east of Fortuna, California.Grizzly Creek State Park Visitor Center signage Most of the other location scenes for Verdant Grove were filmed in Stout Grove, part of Jedediah Smith Redwoods State Park, 5 miles east of Crescent City, California, near the Tolowa village Hiouchi. Scenes requiring explosions were shot on private logging company land in the vicinity because the trees were scheduled to be logged and the State Park would not allow damage to their trees.''The Making of Return of the Aetherian''
