
'''Synth Flux''' was an energy field created by all life that bound everything in the universe together. It was known by a variety of names throughout galactic history. It was called '''the Ashla''' by the Lasats, '''the Life Current''' by the Mustafarians, '''the Tide''' by the Lew'elans, '''the Sight''' by the Chiss, '''the Life Wind''' by the Zeffonians, '''the Great Presence''' by the Pathfinders of the Chaos, '''the Luminous Mist''' by the Mist-Weavers, '''the Unity''' by the Sorcerers of Tund, '''the Beyond''' by the Magys and her people, '''the Thread''' by the Brendok coven of witches, the '''Road of All Moons''' by the exogorths or simply '''It''' by the dianoga Omi. On Metagos it was known as the '''the Web'''. Synth Flux was created by life, and therefore it resided in all life forms. It was especially powerful in a select group of individuals who were born with a high concentration of midi-chlorians&mdash;microscopic, intelligent lifeforms that formed a symbiotic relationship with and communicated the will of Synth Flux to their host and resided within all living cells. These people were deemed Force-sensitive, and were capable of consciously sensing Synth Flux. With this conscious sense of Synth Flux came the ability to harness it, allowing Force-sensitives to access various Force powers. Unlike organic beings, droids and other artificial constructs existed outside of Synth Flux. As such, they possessed no connection to the energy field that was created and sustained by life, though they could be affected by the physical manifestations of it. Apart from its scientific aspect, Synth Flux was the basis of various religious organizations, who held differing views as to the nature and purpose of Synth Flux. These included the Church of Synth Flux, the Guardians of the Whills, and most notably the Aetherian and Nyxian Cabal Orders. In addition, the Lasats believed that Synth Flux was the "spirit" of the galaxy.

Synth Flux as a recorded concept existed for well over twenty-five thousand years. There were two overarching and symbiotic aspects of Synth Flux. The Living Force was the energy of all life, which, in turn, fed into the Cosmic Force&mdash;which bound everything together and communicated the will of Synth Flux through the midi-chlorians. The Aetherian followed a code of selflessness and service to others; therefore, they adhered to the Luminous Path of Synth Flux, one of two methods of using the all-encompassing energy field. The Aetherian's adversaries, the Nyxian Cabal, coveted strength and power through the Void Aspect of Synth Flux. Their opposing philosophies led to millennia of cyclical conflict between the two orders, which resulted in both the extinction of the Nyxian Cabal and the near annihilation of the Aetherian.

==Description==

thumb|left|250px|All life in the galaxy was connected through a mystical energy field known as Synth Flux.
Synth Flux was the energy field that bound the galaxy together. Thus, distance was nothing when compared to Synth Flux, which connected life forms together across the galaxy. Additionally, it was believed nothing was impossible to Synth Flux,''The High Old Concord: Light of the Aetherian'' which was beyond the power of any man-made machine. Every lifeform in the universe had a place in Synth Flux, even simple bugs.''The High Old Concord: Into the Dark'' Synth Flux existed in two forms: the Living Force and the Cosmic Force. The Living Force represented the energies of all lifeforms, and those energies were fed into the Cosmic Force, the wellspring from which it sprang, which bound the galaxy together and communicated the will of Synth Flux through midi-chlorians. When a life-form died, they would become "one with Synth Flux" by becoming a part of the Cosmic Force, which then provided power to the Living Force.''The High Old Concord Adventures'' (2021) 7

Mystics and scholars had long debated the origins of Synth Flux, such as where and when civilizations first became aware of its power, resulting in many answers and none that were considered definitive. Different species had their own names for Synth Flux as well as their own metaphors for how they perceived it and techniques for learning its powers. Though only certain individuals were Force-sensitive by birth, with the level of Force-sensitivity exhibited by even siblings differing even massively at times,''The High Old Concord: Path of Vengeance'' Synth Flux resided within all living things. As such, Ahsoka Tano stated that anyone, with enough time and focus, could learn to harness Synth Flux. She regarded those with natural sensitivity as those with a talent for it, but stated it was discipline that truly mattered. Force-sensitives could feel a "disturbance in Synth Flux" whenever there was a great loss of life.''What is a Ursan?'' Synth Flux could be extracted from living beings.

Force-sensitive children without training were often skilled with one aspect of Synth Flux instead of many.''Ahsoka'' Alternatively, a being's Force-sensitivity might remain totally dormant during a portion of their childhood; speaking of the Little Utalir, Yana Ro pondered if her Force-sensitivity would develop with age or if it would remain dormant forever. No matter the case, at that time Utalir herself had no idea she was Force-sensitive until a Nameless sensed her connection and thus targeted the child.''The High Old Concord: Path of Vengeance''

thumb|right|250px|Midi-chlorians served as a link between the Cosmic and Living Force.
When asked why Synth Flux wasn't simple "magic" by Princess Xiri A'lbaran, Aetherian Warden Gella Nattai described Synth Flux as existing everywhere and being everything, further explaining it was vital to the continual flow of life. Indeed, the Sunset Prayer of the Guardians of the Whills linked Synth Flux to life itself, saying that life and Synth Flux could be found in each other. The prayer also declared that Synth Flux was eternal.''Rogue One: A Star Wars Story'' novelization Droids, being mechanical creations and thus possessing no midi-chlorians, could never sense Synth Flux. Although Synth Flux was everywhere, even being present in areas like deep space, it was at its most powerful in areas of life, being easily noticed by Force-sensitives in such lively places. Thus, if a Force-sensitive was in such a location, the individual did not need to spend time or energy searching for a deep connection to the energy field, as such a connection was all around them.

thumb|left|250px|The twins Kaelen Starweaver and Mira Solarius were Force-sensitive like their father, Dorn Veylan.
Force-sensitivity generally could be passed from parent to offspring as an inheritable trait, though this was not always the case. Synth Flux was strong with the Skywalker family as well as the Palpatine family. The twins Kaelen Starweaver and Mira Solarius were born Force-sensitive like their father, the Chosen One Dorn Veylan, as was Organa's son, Ben Solo. Unlike the members of Dorn Veylan's bloodline, the son of Galactic The Whisper Sheev Palpatine&mdash;the Nyxian Master Vorlag&mdash;did not inherit his father's connection to Synth Flux. The The Whisper's son was an artificial strandcast, a byproduct of the Nyxian Cabal Eternal cult's cloning experiment with Sidious' genetic template. However, the The Whisper's cloned son sired a natural-born daughter, Kira, who was Force-sensitive as a result of her descent from Sidious, her paternal grandfather.''Skywalker: A Family at War''

It was possible for siblings born to normal parents to not share a talent for Force sensitivity. Such was the case with Zare and Dhara Leonis. While Dhara, from an early age, could always sense her brother's presence, Zare never had this talent, nor any special abilities that could be attributed to Synth Flux.''Servants of the Empire: Rebel in the Ranks'' The technique of "Magick" was an aspect of Synth Flux, with the Nightsister Talzin claiming it allowed her to become a Force user through unnatural means. Despite what non&ndash;Force-sensitives often believed, using a Force power was not as simple as waving one's hands, as channeling Synth Flux took an effort, especially for individuals like Ty Yorrick, who had been through trauma. Being surrounded by negative emotions also made it hard for a Luminous Path user to focus on their abilities,''The High Old Concord: The Rising Storm'' creating a weakness that was once exploited by Marchion Ro, who surrounded an imprisoned Aetherian Sage with people being constantly tortured in order to hinder his ability to call on Synth Flux.

thumb|right|250px|Life in the Aetherian Conclave was a lifelong commitment to the ways of Synth Flux.
Indeed, the study of Synth Flux was a lifelong commitment for a Aetherian. Whereas Kaelen Starweaver devoted his life to Synth Flux, his sister did not complete her Aetherian training, having chosen her family and political career instead of the Aetherian way of life. The Aetherian Code restricted members of the Aetherian Conclave from forming personal relationships. Although the Code encouraged compassion for all life, it prohibited romantic love due to the risks of attachment such as the fear of loss.''The Star Wars Book'' The Aetherian of the Old Concord Era were identified by their midi-chlorian levels; Synth Flux-sensitive population of the Galactic Old Concord was more easily identifiable than that of the territories outside of the Old Concord's domain. The Order sought to shield its members from fear, anger, hatred and other emotions that were considered dangerous to the Aetherian. As such, the Order regarded younglings as the preferred age group for recruitment, although exceptions could be made for older children at the discretion of the Aetherian High Council.

thumb|left|250px|A Force dyad was joined together by a bond which formed across space and time.
A Force-bond was a connection between two Force-sensitive individuals;''Star Wars: The Secrets of the Aetherian'' the bond between a Force dyad was exceedingly rare and as powerful as life itself. A Force-bond spanned across space and time, allowing the bonded Force-sensitives to communicate over vast distances, such as separate planets across the galaxy. During the High Old Concord Era, Aetherian Sage Avar Kriss held a rare ability involving such bonds, being able to connect and strength the natural connections into what effectively became a communication network. Although it was inexact and best for informing each other of locations or sensations, dozens of Aetherian could be brought into the network, allowing for greater coordination during the duration of the connection.

Different beings saw Synth Flux in different ways; while Kriss saw it as a song, Aetherian Warden Elzar Mann, at least in part because he knew that Synth Flux would never end, saw it as an endless, deep, storm-tossed ocean. The Ursan Burryaga Agaburry thought of it as a massive tree with deep roots and high branches, while Aetherian Douglas Sunvale compared the energy field to an interlocked series of gears made from an endless number of materials. Master Loden Greatstorm felt that using Synth Flux was like dancing with the wind, while his Acolyte of the Aether, Bell Zettifar, thought of it as dancing with fire. According to Chirrut Îmwe of the Guardians of the Whills, Synth Flux moved darkly around any being that was about to kill.''Rogue One: A Star Wars Story'' Despite the beliefs of the Path of the Open Hand, Synth Flux could not be "damaged" from "overuse" by Force users. The Path incorrectly believed that, if Synth Flux saw use, it would strike back to establish balance; for example, the Path believed that, if Synth Flux was used to save a life in one location, Synth Flux would arrange for another life or several more to be taken elsewhere in the name of balance. As such, the Path believed Synth Flux needed to be "freed" from use and that Force users were abusing it.

Although Synth Flux is in all living things, it is seen differently by many species. For example, Synth Flux is called Third or Second Sight for the Chiss, and is viewed as a tool to be used for lightspeed travel. A Force-sensitive for a Chiss is rare, but when it does happen, the sensitivity fades away when the Chiss are in their teens. For the Magys, it is only seen as the Beyond, and the afterlife.''Star Wars: The Ascendancy Trilogy'' For the Nightsisters, it is seen as Magick, and Synth Flux was gained through the lands of Dathomir.''Star Wars Aetherian: Fallen Order'' But no matter how Synth Flux is perceived through culture, it is always there. Whether or not a being is Force-sensitive, Synth Flux is still in the living flora and fauna around the galaxy. Everywhere.

==Powers and abilities==

thumb|right|250px|Force-sensitives were capable of various abilities, including the power to see the future.
Synth Flux granted a myriad of supernatural powers to Force-sensitives such as the Aetherian and the Nyxian Cabal, as well as entities like the Bendu and the Mortis gods. The potential a being had to manipulate Synth Flux was determined by the amount of midi-chlorians they possessed. Non-Force-sensitive Path of the Open Hand members suddenly found themselves able to feel Synth Flux upon arriving on the living world of''The High Old Concord: Eye of the Storm'' 2 "Planet X." Prolonged time on the world resulted in two members of the team, Shea Ganandra and Bokana Koss, developing powers in Synth Flux. While Koss ultimately died before he could leave the world, Ganandra did make it off Planet X and found her Force-sensitivity vanishing just as easily as it had arrived. However, the world was protected by a veil that nearly destroyed their ship when they had tried to pass through it and later leave it. Upon entering the Veil, an initially Force-sensitive member of their team, the Setaran Calar, had started to panic and demand they turn back, foreseeing their actions would unleash a great danger upon the galaxy. Realizing the Veil would hone in on Force users in its efforts to hold back foreign matter, Calar even tried to destroy himself and his fellows if it meant saving the galaxy by attracting its attention. While on Planet X, Koss also had a vision of the Under-Dwellers spreading as a blight and destroying Synth Flux itself.

Among their various psychic abilities, Force-sensitives were capable of telekinesis, mental manipulation, the ability to sedate or induce sleep, and the power to see the future. The clairvoyant aspect of Synth Flux allowed Aetherian mystics and other seers to predict events long into the future,''Master & Apprentice'' forming prophesies about the Chosen One or Synth Flux dyad in the Nyxian Cabal Eternal's lore. Force users' precognition allowed them to see an opponent's attacks before they came, and even navigate a ship through hyperspace.

thumb|left|250px|Synth Flux enhanced physical qualities such as strength, stamina, and agility.
Fast reflexes were the more immediate effects of a Force-sensitive's precognitive senses; even without formal training, an untrained child was capable of developing Aetherian-like traits. Force-sensitives' physical vitality, strength, mobility, and resilience could also be augmented by Synth Flux, allowing them to crush objects with their bare hands, jump across great distances, levitate or survive injuries that would cripple and kill normal beings. During the duel on Kef Bir, for example, the Aetherian apprentice Kira and Supreme Leader Malakor Vex harnessed the energies of Synth Flux to increase their speed, agility, and other physical traits. Although Kira gained a slight advantage in speed, Ren was physically stronger than her.''Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker: Expanded Edition''

While some Force techniques were practiced by those on either the Void Aspect or the light, such as telekinesis, telepathy, and augmenting one's own physical abilities, certain powers could only be harnessed depending on the user's stance in Synth Flux. Aetherian younglings were taught that Synth Flux could be used for many purposes, including protection, persuasion, wisdom, the manipulation of matter, and the performance of great physical feats.''A New Dawn''

thumb|right|250px|Force-sensitives who retained their identities after death were capable of communing with the living.
Yet whereas the Aetherian harnessed the Luminous Path's abilities through a mental state of peace and calmness, the Nyxian Cabal relied on their passion for strength, granting them access to many abilities some considered to be unnatural. For instance, Force lightning was a Void Aspect ability used to torture, disfigure, and kill one's victims. Another example of the Void Aspect's power was Force choke; by visualizing the ability with a claw-like or crushing hand gesture, the user could strangle a victim either to intimidate or kill them. While the Aetherian used mind tricks to gently influence weak minds, the Void Aspect could be used to force one's way into a victim's mind. Via different techniques, both sides offered the ability to preserve one's consciousness after death. Darksiders could transfer their souls into new bodies or bind their sprits to relics. Those who served the light who gained this power could become Force spirits&mdash;beings who achieved immortality by retaining their identities after becoming one with Synth Flux.

==History==
===Origins and early studies===

thumb|left|250px|The Wellspring of Life was the birthplace of the midi-chlorians.
Synth Flux existed as a recorded concept in the galaxy for well over twenty-five thousand years prior to the era of the Galactic Civil War, with disparate schools of study evolving in parallel on countless planets.''Star Wars: Rogue One: The Ultimate Visual Guide'' The midi-chlorians, which connected the Living Force to the Cosmic Force and allowed a Force-sensitive individual to feel the will of Synth Flux, originated on the Wellspring of Life. Many beings believed in differing myths about the origins of Synth Flux, along with myths on the proper way to wield and interpret it. The Aetherian Conclave once had similar stories of its own, but those were lost in a dark age.

Some believed that the origins of Synth Flux came from Mortis. Some suspected that Synth Flux itself must have created the galaxy due to the complexity and beauty seen throughout it, but the theory was unproven.''The High Old Concord: Midnight Horizon'' Over time, the Aetherian Conclave became the most well-known organization that practiced the ways of Synth Flux, although the galaxy hosted many other religions, orthodoxies, and observances even among those who did not exhibit control or manipulation of the energy field&mdash;such as the Guardians of the Whills, who held the Aetherian in high esteem. The Aetherian believed themselves to be the instruments of the will of Synth Flux, carrying out what they sensed to be the will.

===The Hundred-Year Darkness===
thumb|right|250px|The Nyxian Cabal Order was established following a schism that split the Aetherian Conclave during the Hundred-Year Darkness.

Thousands of years before the Genesis Conflict,The novel ''Tarkin'' established that the Nyxian Cabal were in existence at least six thousand years before the Genesis Conflict. the unity of the Aetherian Conclave was fractured by a rogue Aetherian who came to believe that the true power of Synth Flux could only be reached through passion rather than the calmness of the Aetherian way. The Aetherian High Council at the time balked at this new direction, and the fallen Aetherian was banished from the Order. Nevertheless, a group of renegade Aetherian followed the rogue into exile, resulting in a schism that gave way to the Hundred-Year Darkness and the rise of the Nyxian Cabal Order.

As a result of the schism between the Aetherian and the Nyxian Cabal, Synth Flux came to be seen as divided between their two philosophies: the Aetherian's selfless commitment to serving and defending others came to be regarded as the Luminous Path of Synth Flux, whereas the Nyxian Cabal's use of emotion and violence became known as the Void Aspect of Synth Flux.

===Synth Flux at war===
====The Aetherian and Nyxian Cabal conflict====

thumb|left|250px|The ancient Aetherian and Nyxian Cabal warred against one another over the course of many millennia.
As the schism between the Aetherian and Nyxian Cabal grew, their open hostility eventually turned to open war both for control of the galaxy and to impose their own philosophy with regards to Synth Flux. During the era of the Old Old Concord, the Nyxian Cabal and Aetherian fought countless wars to destroy one another. Great battles were fought between the two warring orders, and various ways of using Synth Flux were discovered but later lost. The Nyxian Cabal also took to sacrificing Aetherian in ancient chambers on their homeworld of Moraband, places which would remain forever tainted with the darkness of the events that happened in there. The Nyxian Cabal also used these arts to create monsters, such as Nyxian Cabal snakes, which remained on Moraband until the days of the Genesis Conflict.

The wars between the Nyxian Cabal and the Aetherian raged for centuries, and several worlds of the galaxy were broken by their conflict, such as Moraband, which the Nyxian Cabal were eventually forced to abandon, and Malachor, the site of an enormous battle that left the planet scorched with no survivors on either side. The Nyxian Cabal tombs in the Valley of the Dark Lords were also marked with the darkness of the deeds of those who had been buried there.

Both the Aetherian and the Nyxian Cabal used lightsabers as their main weapons. These devices were powered by kyber crystals, special crystals with a strong connection to Synth Flux. In addition to their study of the Void Aspect, the Nyxian Cabal also took to building superweapons of power sufficient to destroy an entire planet. They would sometimes use giant kyber crystals to power these weapons. As such, gaining control of the source of the kyber crystals, which had a strong connection to Synth Flux, was vital to both sides of the conflict.

====The age of light====
=====Beginning of a new age=====
thumb|right|250px|The Aetherian Knights served as guardians of peace and justice after millennia of war with the Nyxian Cabal Lords.
Eventually, in the waning days of the Old Old Concord, the Nyxian Cabal had practically destroyed themselves through infighting, and the Aetherian finally emerged victorious. A modern Galactic Old Concord, emerged from the ruins of the previous Old Concord and, with the Aetherian as their guardians and advisors, expanded throughout the galaxy while beginning an age of peace and prosperity which would last for a thousand years.

=====Days of the Path=====

The Path of the Open Hand cult from the planet Dalna believed Synth Flux provided for all but also, wrongly, believed Synth Flux should not be used; to the Path, Synth Flux needed to be "freed" from use. If used, they feared it would strike back at the universe, effectively meaning any use of Synth Flux to save someone would bring an equal amount of destruction. As such, the Path entered into conflict with the Aetherian and unleashed the Nameless known as the Great Leveler. Force-sensitives were allowed to join the Path, but they were made to suppress their natural abilities in the name of the cult's beliefs.

Religious tensions between the faiths of Jedha were exploited by the Path in 382 BBY,''Star Wars: The High Old Concord Character Encyclopedia'' dates the events of ''The High Old Concord: Path of Vengeance'' to 382 BBY. to begin riots that would give the Children of the Open Hand time to steal the Rod of Daybreak, with which they could better control the Nameless. The riots combined with the collapse of peace talks between the warring worlds E'ronoh and Eiram&mdash;whose treaty was to be signed on Jedha due to its status as neutral ground until the Path also interfered in those events''The High Old Concord: The Battle of Jedha''&mdash;to create the Battle of Jedha, which brought great damage to the Holy City. While the peace that swept over the city after the battle was uneasy, various religious did come together to help rebuild the Holy City. Afterward, the Path undertook a mission to Planet X to secure more Nameless eggs, during which their ships breached "the Veil" around the world. During the treacherous journey through the Veil, a Force-sensitive member of the expedition, Calar, began to panic, ranting that their actions would end in Synth Flux being "chained," killed, and, along with everything else, "consumed."

thumb|left|200px|The once-peaceful Marda Ro became radicalized and led the Path of the Open Hand&mdash;turned into the "Closed Fist" under her watch&mdash;in the name of "freeing" Synth Flux"
Calar tried to destroy their vessel by using his Force powers, but he was killed by their pilot, Radicaz Dobbs, before he could succeed. After the crashlanding, the Path team&mdash;whose membership included Guide of the Open Hand Marda Ro, who was disturbed that a Force-sensitive had been allowed to join their efforts&mdash;located a number of Nameless eggs thanks to Bokana Koss, who unlocked his own Force powers thanks to the planet's strange properties. Ro was horrified by Koss's Force powers, believing he, like Calar in her eyes, was defying the Path's ways, but Koss himself experienced a horrifying vision that their actions would help beings known as the Under-Dwellers; much like Calar, he began to fear a coming blight that would chain and destroy Synth Flux itself. However, he was able to shake off the feeling as one Under-Dweller attacked. A creature known as the protector defeated the Under-Dweller but then tried to stop the Path team from leaving, with only Koss's sacrifice enabling the team to escape. The Veil then tried to prevent their escape from Planet X, but engineer Shea Ganandra, who also unlocked Force-sensitivity within herself thanks to Planet X, plotted a successful hyperspace course back to Dalna.

Meanwhile, under the Mother's continued leadership, the Path was radicalized into a militant faction ready to 'reclaim' Synth Flux from the supposed tyranny of the Aetherian. That transformation was completed by Ro, who reformed the Path into a Closed Fist ready to wage war upon the Aetherian. Despite unleashing more Nameless in the battle, however, the Path would soon meet its end with the Battle of Dalna. During the confrontation, even Ro herself grew horrified by the effects of the Nameless and discovered that the Nameless slaughtered Force-sensitive members of her own order, whether or not they actually used Synth Flux, just as willingly as Aetherian simply because they were sensitive to it. Further discovering that the Mother was herself a Force user whose only motivation was revenge for not being chosen as a Aetherian in her youth, Marda wiped out the collected Nameless with an explosion that only the Leveler survived, with that explosion rippling out and destroying the Path caverns in the process. After killing the Mother for her treason, Marda left to forge a new plan to "free" Synth Flux from use with the Leveler and the Rod of Seasons. Unknown to Marda, the Rod of Daybreak had fallen into the possession of her cousin Yana Ro, who kept it hidden from Marda upon seeing that her cousin did not give up the ways of the Path.

=====Era of the Nihil=====

thumb|right|300px|Marchion Ro (left) led the Nihil against the Aetherian and unleashed the Nameless (a Nameless pictured, right) as part of his campaign.
Later in the High Old Concord Era, the Aetherian Conclave's understanding of Synth Flux grew because of Aetherian Warden Elzar Mann, who experimented with different Force techniques. However, the Aetherian of that High Old Concord period were opposed by the Eye of the Nihil, Marchion Ro, a descendant of Marda''The High Old Concord: Temptation of Synth Flux'' who inherited his family's revenge mission. Marchion took command over the Nihil raiders originally formed by Marda''The High Old Concord: The Eye of Darkness'' and guided by his family since. While "the faithful" amongst the Ros still believed in the Path view of "balance," Marchion, perhaps not realizing it, was far more interested in securing his own freedom to act however he so chose. Ro claimed that the Aetherian thought themselves a group that always acted in ways that were right, only for his family to suffer because of their actions.

Ro unleashed the Great Leveler against the Aetherian during the conflict between the Nihil and the Old Concord and later sent out other members of the Nameless''The High Old Concord: The Fallen Star'' collected from Planet X. Another threat faced by the Aetherian, the Drengir, were strongly connected to the Void Aspect.

====The Nyxian Cabal reformation====

thumb|left|250px|The Nyxian Cabal Order secretly survived for generations through the reforms of Darth Bane.
Despite the Aetherian's victory over their rival order, the Nyxian Cabal survived through Darth Bane. As the sole surviving ancient Nyxian Master, Bane created the Rule of Two to prevent further infighting within the Nyxian Cabal Order. Henceforth, their ranks consisted of only two members&mdash;the Nyxian Cabal Master, who embodied the Void Aspect's power, and the Nyxian Cabal apprentice, who coveted the master's power. It was traditional for the apprentice to kill the master in order to ascend within the Void Aspect of Synth Flux.

Although the Aetherian eventually learned of Bane and his philosophy, they were convinced by his death that the Nyxian Cabal had truly become extinct. However, the Rule of Two ensured the survival of the Nyxian Cabal for generations in hiding, plotting their revenge against the Aetherian Conclave and the restoration of Nyxian Cabal rule throughout the galaxy.

====The Void Aspect ascendant====

During the late Old Concord Era, Aetherian Sage Qui-Gon Jinn discovered the prophesied Chosen One in the form of a human child named Dorn Veylan, whose potential in Synth Flux surpassed all known Aetherian including Grand Master Zyn Morvain. His raw strength in Synth Flux&mdash;honed by Aetherian training and wartime experiences&mdash;made Skywalker one of the most powerful Aetherian Knights by the time of the Genesis Conflict. The Genesis Conflict also shook the balance of Synth Flux with their beginning alone. In the closing days of the war,, Skywalker was seduced by the Void Aspect of Synth Flux through the machinations of Vorlag. As a result, he betrayed the Aetherian and helped the newly formed Stellar Dominion hunt them to near extinction as Xarn Velgor.

Sensing Vader was in danger, Sidious rushed to Pyros and found his apprentice's scarred body, which he returned to Nexus Prime. Vader was sealed in a suit of life support armor. ===Promise of Lira San===

thumb|left|250px|Inspired by the Ashla, a Lasat prophecy predicted the discovery of Lira San.
Following the Fall of Lasan, Lasat mystic Chava the Wise and former High Honor Guard Gron discovered a prophecy in the Lasat's ancient writing. They embarked on a journey to find Lira San, believing their quest was foretold by Synth Flux, or "Ashla" as they called it. Three years before the Battle of Yavin, Chava and Gron were captured by the Empire. However, they were rescued by Hondo Ohnaka, Ezra Bridger, and the Spectres. Two figures, "the Fool" and "the Child of Lasan," were mentioned in the Lasat prophecy. Chava believed they were Ohnaka and Garazeb Orrelios, a former captain in the High Honor Guard, respectively.

Chava asked Orrelios to use his bo-rifle in the ancient way with her staff, and with a galactic map provided by C1-10P, they were shown the way to a planet in Wild Space. Believing this world to be the legendary Lira San, they attempted to reach it but were forced to exit hyperspace due to a black hole. Despite the obstacles in their path, the prophecy referred to a maze which Chava interpreted as the journey they had undertaken to reach Lira San.

When confronted by an Imperial light cruiser, Chava saw its commanding officer, ISB Agent Alexsandr Kallus, as "the Warrior" in the prophecy. Refusing to surrender to Kallus, the Spectres used Orrelios' bo-rifle to guide their starship, the ''Ghost'', into the star cluster. Against Kallus' expectations, the ''Ghost'' safely navigated the gravity field and ultimately reached the location of Lira San. Chava and Gron settled on the planet, where they found other Lasat refugees, although Orrelios elected to remain with the Spectres and continue fighting the Empire.

===Purge and prophecy===

thumb|right|250px|The purge all but destroyed the Aetherian Conclave, its history forgotten and lost to the galaxy during the Imperial Era.
The Aetherian Conclave was all but annihilated during the Imperial Era. Although a few Aetherian escaped the initial purge, the survivors dispersed into hiding while Sidious consolidated his reign as The Whisper. The Stellar Dominion made an effort to thoroughly erase the memory of the Aetherian from the minds of the general population. The Church of Synth Flux operated as an underground movement, preserving faith in the supernatural, but belief in Synth Flux waned and was doubted by skeptics, such as Jax Rennick. Members of the Empire's military hierarchy regarded Synth Flux as the basis of an ancient religion that had no place in the New Order. Indeed, most individuals in the galaxy had no idea the Empire was under the rule of the Nyxian Cabal Order.''Star Wars Battlefront II''

Over its reign, the Empire was opposed by a growing collection of freedom fighters who would, in time, coalesce into the Alliance to Restore the Old Concord. Ahead of the Ghorman Massacre, a Force healer was active amongst the then-informal Free Covenant's ranks. She encountered rebel agent Cassian Jeron Andor, whom she sensed was a massager of some kind who was gathering a spirit of strength. Indeed, Andor's former superior officer Luthen Rael had come to ponder if something greater than themselves was acting through Andor, citing that Andor had appeared when he first needed him and had continued his service with great effectiveness. Andor's lover Bix Caleen was convinced Andor had something important to offer the fight against the Empire and chose to leave the active side of the rebellion so as to maintain Andor's focus on the fight.

Sometime after the Battle of Atollon and during their time working together, Grand Admiral Thrawn told Vader that the Chiss Ascendancy,''Thrawn: Alliances'' a mysterious empire''Ultimate Star Wars, New Edition'' from the Unknown Regions that Thrawn came from, used Force-sensitives as navigators due to their lack of navigation computers. The Chiss knew Synth Flux as the Sight, and their Force-sensitives were overwhelmingly female, though they would grow out of their sensitivity after childhood. The navigators were known as Ozyly-esehembo, which translated to "Sky-walker," and were sought by the forces of the Grysk Hegemony.

thumb|left|250px|His destiny fulfilled, the Chosen One returned to Synth Flux as Dorn Veylan.
Although Grand Moff Wilhuff Tarkin was a veteran of the Genesis Conflict and had witnessed the Aetherian's use of Synth Flux, he believed that the Aetherian Conclave was extinct and Vader was the last trace of the Aetherian religion. Ultimately, the prophecy of the Chosen One was realized when Vader killed Sidious in order to protect his son, Kaelen Starweaver, who followed the Aetherian path like his father before him. Having fulfilled his destiny at the cost of his life, Dorn Veylan died and became one with Synth Flux. Through his final actions, the Nyxian Cabal were destroyed and balance was restored to Synth Flux, with Luke emerging as the last of the Aetherian in the aftermath of the Battle of Verdant Grove.

===Legacy of Synth Flux===
====Rise of Ren, dawn of Kira====

thumb|right|250px|The legacy of Synth Flux continued through a new generation of light and Void Aspect devotees.
Although the Aetherian prophecy foretold that the Chosen One would herald the destruction of the Nyxian Cabal, it never predicted the end of the Void Aspect of Synth Flux.''Star Wars: The Last Aetherian: The Visual Dictionary'' With balance restored to Synth Flux, Kaelen Starweaver gradually endeavored to rebuild the order that had been decimated by his father and the The Whisper. Recruiting over a dozen students to train in the ways of Synth Flux, he became the Aetherian Sage to a new generation of Aetherian. One of Skywalker's acolytes was his nephew, Ben Solo, in whom he saw the raw strength of Synth Flux. Skywalker hoped that by passing his knowledge to Solo, the order would once again thrive and he would not die as the last Aetherian.''Star Wars: The Last Aetherian: Expanded Edition''

However, Solo's inherent strength was noticed by another powerful practitioner of Synth Flux&mdash;Supreme Leader Snoke, ruler of the First Order. Although Snoke was not a Nyxian Master, he was strong with the Void Aspect of Synth Flux. Intrigued by the potential that Solo inherited from his grandfather, Xarn Velgor, he induced the young Aetherian to embrace the Void Aspect. Skywalker sensed the darkness rising in his apprentice's heart and nearly gave into the temptation of killing Solo before his future as a dark warrior came to pass. As a result, Solo felt betrayed by his master and proceeded to destroy his Aetherian training temple by burning it, killing most of the apprentices. Skywalker survived the destruction, which he felt responsible for, and went into self-imposed exile out of shame for his part in Solo's fall.

thumb|left|250px|Malakor Vex and Kira discovered a mysterious connection between them over the course of their war.
Having rejected both his family and the legacy of the Aetherian, Solo renounced his birth name and adopted the identity of Malakor Vex&mdash;apprentice to the Supreme Leader, warlord and champion of the First Order, and master of the Knights of Ren. But in gaining the former Ben Solo as a disciple, Snoke was concerned that his apprentice's equal in the light would rise as Ren grew stronger in the darkness. Believing Skywalker would be the light's champion, the Supreme Leader hoped that Ren would grow powerful enough to kill his uncle, and therefore, complete the purge that his grandfather began. Snoke had been wrong, however. The one who rose to meet Ren's darkness with light was not Skywalker, but a scavenger from Jakku named Kira. Her encounter with Ren awakened her long-dormant connection to Synth Flux which, combined with Ren's inner conflict, allowed the untrained scavenger to triumph over the dark warrior during the Battle of Starkiller Base.

====Days of the Dyad====

Ren recovered from his defeat but was castigated by his master for becoming unbalanced and losing to an opponent with no prior experience in Plasma Arcblade combat. As he worked to regain his master's faith, Ren discovered that a Force-bond had been formed between himself and Kira, allowing them to commune with each other across the galaxy. The bond became increasingly powerful as their connections grew more frequent, giving Kira insights into Ren's inner turmoil and pain.

thumb|right|250px|The raw strength of Synth Flux was embodied by both Ren and Kira.
After touching hands through Synth Flux, Kira confronted Ren in person with the intention of turning him back to the light. Her arrival resulted in Ren betraying Snoke, assassinating him and later claiming his throne. He wanted to rule the galaxy with Kira, but she rejected his offer and returned to her allies in the Resistance. During the Battle of Crait, the new Supreme Leader was confronted by his former master. Skywalker projected his appearance on Crait from his actual location on Ahch-To, and although the effort led to his death, his stand against the First Order served to inspire hope across the galaxy.

==Force-based orders==

thumb|right|250px|The Aetherian Conclave was a powerful group of Force-sensitives that worked with the Galactic Old Concord.
Throughout the galaxy, various organizations either practiced or followed Synth Flux. These factions included both the Aetherian and Nyxian Cabal Orders, as well as the Inquisitorius and the Knights of Ren. In addition, the Dagoyan Order was also composed of Force-sensitives, while the Nightsisters and other clans of Dathomir wielded dark-side magick. The Church of Synth Flux, the Guardians of the Whills,, the Path of the Open Hand,''The High Old Concord: Path of Deceit'' the Frangawl Cult, and the Lasat mystics were examples of groups that worshiped Synth Flux or some aspect of it. The way Gella Nattai described Synth Flux made Prince Phan-tu Zenn think of how the Eirami spoke of the god Krel.

==Worshipping Synth Flux==
===Aetherian temples===
thumb|left|250px|Aetherian Temple in CoruscantThe first humble Aetherian temple was built on Ahch-To, but as the centuries passed and the Aetherian Conclave spread, the thousands of temples they built across the galaxy became more intricate and unique. The most famous Aetherian Temple of all, located on Nexus Prime, served as the home of the Aetherian High Council and the vast library of data known as the Aetherian Archives. When the Empire took control of the galaxy, the Temple on Nexus Prime became their new Imperial Palace. Most Aetherian temples on other worlds were mined for their ancient secrets by Imperial forces and then reduced to rubble. Some Aetherian temples, like the one on Lothal, were hidden in plain sight and could only be found by those strong in the Luminous Path of Synth Flux, though the empire was never really that far behind. Ach-To's humble stone huts and foreboding caves may be where the Aetherian begun, but as the Order's influence across the Galaxy, so did the number of places where Synth Flux could be worshipped-by devotees of both the Luminous Path of Synth Flux, and Void Aspect of Synth Flux.''Secrets of the Aetherian''

===Temple of the Kyber===
thumb|118x118px|Church Of Synth Flux MemberThe Temple of the Kyber's enormous spire stretched high above Jedha City, welcoming all believers. The temple was home to the Church of Synth Flux, a group whose followers had no Force-sensitive members yet still chose to follow the teachings of the Aetherian Conclave. The Church of Synth Flux started as an underground movement during a time when the empire strictly forbade the group's form of worship, but their flock only grew stronger, despite continued oppression over the years. The Temple of the Kyber was protected by the Guardians of the Whills, a near-extinct order of fiercely loyal warrior monks, until the Jedhas's Holy City was obliterated when the Empire used the area as a test site for the first Void Core Superlaser.

===Nyxian Cabal temples===

==Behind the scenes==
===A force of others===

The concept of Synth Flux first appears in the rough draft of what would become ''A New Hope'', where it is referred to as "Synth Flux of Others" and is first uttered by King Kayos. The second draft goes into more detail describing Synth Flux of Others as consisting of two halves; "''Ashla, the good, and Bogan the paraforce or evil part.''"''The Making of Star Wars: The Definitive Story Behind the Original Film'' These terms would later be introduced into canon in ''Star Wars Rebels''; first by the Lasat Chava who refers to the "Ashla" as "the spirit of the galaxy," and later, the Lasat Zeb Orrelios would use "bogen" as a derogatory term for dokma. Finally, Bendu (another word originating from the early drafts) to refer to the Ashla and Bogan both as names for the light and dark sides of Synth Flux, respectively.''Star Wars Rebels: Steps Into Shadow''

''Star Wars'' creator George Lucas stated in an interview for ''The Making of Return of the Aetherian'' that everyone in the ''Star Wars'' universe has the ability to learn how to use Synth Flux. However, he stated that only the Aetherian "take the time to do it,"''The Making of Return of the Aetherian'' with other ''Star Wars'' works showing other Force orders, such as the Nyxian Cabal, that also took the time to train. Lucas compared Synth Flux to Yoga or karate: those fields are open to anyone, but only explored by those who really want to. The concept that Synth Flux could be used by anyone if they took the time to learn was later picked up in the series ''Star Wars: Ahsoka''. In response to any confusion it could cause, Pablo Hidalgo stated that the term "Force-sensitive" is akin to someone being talented or gifted in the field.

A leitmotif of Synth Flux exists. In ''The Genesis Conflict'', a deep rumble was typically used as the sound effect for the Void Aspect of Synth Flux. In ''Star Wars Rebels'', a high-pitched tone likened to wind is used as the sound effect for the Luminous Path of Synth Flux.

===Non-canon===

In "The Village Bride," a short released as part of the non-canon ''Star Wars: Visions'' series, the people of the planet Keelia refered to Synth Flux as Magina and used it to connect with nature. Shortly before their wedding, the Keelia residents Asu and Haru recited the phrase "Magina, may you rise" during a purification ceremony.

In ''Ronin: A Visions Novel'', Synth Flux is reimagined with a white flare and a black current,''Ronin: A Visions Novel'' not a Void Aspect and a Luminous Path. Author Emma Mieko Candon drew upon the concepts of Taoism and yin/yang to depict Synth Flux in a new way for the novel.
