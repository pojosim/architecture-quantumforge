'''Droids''', less commonly known as '''robots''' and '''automatons''', were mechanical beings that possessed artificial intelligence. They were used in a variety of roles and environments, often those considered too menial or too dangerous for other species, but also in fields that required extensive specialization and knowledge.

==History==

The earliest origins of droids were lost to history, and, by the time of the New Republic, it was unknown exactly when the first droid was created,''Star Wars: Droidography'' though some historians, only taking into account the more advanced worlds, placed the origin of the first droids to approximately 30,000 years before the Battle of Yavin. Nevertheless, for millennia, droids served in galactic society alongside organic beings.''Star Wars Propaganda: A History of Persuasive Art in the Galaxy'' According to the B1-series battle droid author R0-GR, the bond between organic and mechanical lifeforms were tenuous with the former fearing that droids would rise up against their masters. Though several droid revolts took place, Roger claimed that an "unspoken understanding" between human and droid cultures kept everyone working on the same team throughout the ages.

Droids existed as far back as the earliest days of the Jedi Order, which was founded over 25,000 years before the Clone Wars.''Tarkin'' One such ancient droid, Professor Huyang, served the Jedi for several millennia. As a result of this ancient coexistence with living beings, the galaxy often had an uneven relationship between organics and mechanicals. Records of uprisings and clashes between droids and their organic overlords became increasingly common as droids slowly became more important in galactic society. During the High Republic Era, the Nihil welcomed droids into their ranks.''The High Republic: Tempest Runner'' They also designed blaredroids and scav droids as weapons.''The High Republic: The Rising Storm''

thumb|270x270px|left|During the Clone Wars, the Confederacy deployed a massive army of various battle-droid models. The extensive use of droids in combat led to an increase in anti-droid sentiment.
During the pan-galactic conflict known as the Clone Wars, the Confederacy of Independent Systems used a variety of battle droids in its fight against the Galactic Republic. This mass use of droids in combat cemented the idea of the "droid as an existential threat to biological life" narrative in the minds of the Republic. Droids were not utilized by the Chiss Ascendancy by the time of the Clone Wars.''Thrawn Ascendancy: Chaos Rising'' Ultimately, the Republic won the war and was replaced by an authoritarian Galactic Empire. Under the new regime, many people in the galaxy were afraid or otherwise distrustful of droids, due to their bad memories of the conflict.''Star Wars'': Card Trader

The Galactic Empire fostered anti-droid sentiment and treated droids as property. Many sentients continued the Clone Wars policies of wiping droids' memories, fitting them with restraining bolts, and curtailing their movements. Several establishments also excluded droids. Further, anti-droid sentiment adversely affected the labor fields dedicated to droid design and manufacture, which forced those specialized in those fields&mdash;such as Maela's mother&mdash;to begin working in Imperial droid factories in order to maintain a job. Otherwise, there were simply not enough openings in the market for those skills. After the Clone Wars, the Imperial Senate also passed a law prohibiting programming that would allow for the harming of organics in the droids used by the Empire.''Ultimate Star Wars, New Edition'' Thus, with both public opinion and the law against it, battle droid production fell sharply.''Reign of the Empire: The Mask of Fear'' However, this law was bypassed by Arakyd Industries in conjunction with the Imperial Military by marketing their KX-series droids as "security droids".

The illegal sport of droid-fighting was practiced on Nal Hutta''Star Wars'': Outer Rim and in the Lodge, where Clone Wars-survivor Ralakili ran the fighting pits due to his hate of droids.''Solo: A Star Wars Story The Official Guide'' The Rebel Alliance, which opposed the Empire, treated droids not as property but as sentient beings.''Star Wars: The Last Jedi: The Visual Dictionary'' Several droids were encouraged by the Rebellion and carried out acts of resistance such as bypassing repressive programming, deactivating restraining bolts, and demanding freedom. The Rebellion's acceptance of droids as equals was continued by the New Republic and Resistance. Support Droids were units that existed at some stage of galactic history that provided various assistant roles in battle.''Star Wars: Galaxy of Heroes''

==Definition==
===Mechanical "beings"===

thumb|right|270px|Various droid models existed for many roles.
Droids were mechanical constructs designed to assist organic lifeforms in various tasks. Depending on the nature of the work they performed, they possessed various degrees of artificial intelligence.''Ultimate Star Wars'' Most droids had hard-coded restrictions against harming organics. Notable exceptions included security droids''Star Wars: Commander'' and assassin droids. It was the nature of less complex droids to assume that if a human acted as if they belonged somewhere, then that human did belong there.''Master & Apprentice''

Droids came in all sizes and shapes, and many of them resembled humanoid organics, possessing an upright posture, two arms, two legs, hands, feet, one thorax, a neck, and a head.''Star Wars: Absolutely Everything You Need to Know'' The B1 battle droids were designed to resemble their insectoid Geonosian creators. Likewise, the Colicoids designed the three-legged droidekas in their image.''Star Wars Character Encyclopedia: Updated and Expanded'' Some droids, like the LOM-series unit, were made to resemble the organics they served.

Contrary to organics, who were born and healed slowly,''What is a Wookiee?'' droids were easily built en masse, were less fragile, and could be repaired easily.''Darth Vader'' (2015) 18 They also did not have to learn skills (such as calculation abilities), as they were directly programmed with them. Most droids possessed a form of self-awareness, with advanced models even having emotions such as fear for their existence. Droids did not need to breathe,''The High Republic Adventures – The Nameless Terror'' 1 eat or drink.''Star Wars: The Visual Encyclopedia'' They were capable of surviving in a vacuum and underwater, and were immune to cosmic radiation, all poisons, toxins''Knights of Fate'' and diseases. They did not need to sleep, although they could be deactivated as a form of sleep and had occasional maintenance cycles.''Servants of the Empire: Imperial Justice''

===Not "alive" in an organic sense===

thumb|left|250px|0-0-0 theorized that a special engine powered by human blood could connect a droid to the Force.
Droids were broadly considered not to be alive. They could not be detected by scanners designed to detect the presence of life-forms. Similarly, Droids could not access the Force, an energy field that connected all living things in the galaxy. Additionally, not being alive, droids could not be sensed in the Force, though a skilled Jedi might sense their presence through the empty spots they left in the Force. However, R0-GR nonetheless referred to droids as mechanical life-forms and artificial lives. The reprogrammed protocol unit Triple-Zero, theorized that the Force might flow through a droid equipped with a special engine driven by human blood.

They could not die in a traditional sense, though they could be destroyed. If enough of the droid survived, they could be repaired, however.'''' Droids were usually completely obedient and precise, and normally followed their proprietors' orders. However, unlike mere machines or primitive computers, droids were capable of developing unique personalities by gathering experiences. They could form friendships, and experience emotions such as happiness, pain, and sadness.''Star Wars Builders: Droids'' To avoid that, most droid owners performed regular memory wipes on their properties. However, memory wipes were not always perfect, occasionally leaving droids with vague recollections.''Star Wars Adventures: Tales from Vader's Castle'' 1

===The droid rights debate===

Even so, some droids were emancipated.''Star Wars: Rogue One: The Ultimate Visual Guide'' Movements for droid rights existed throughout the galaxy, promoting equality between droids and other sentient beings,''Solo: A Star Wars Story: Tales from Vandor'' with droids such as L3-37 believing strongly in this cause.

==Classes of droids==
===Overview===

thumb|right|200px|L3-37 was a custom, self-made droid from many different droid parts
Droids could be entrusted with a multitude of tasks, ranging from running elementary diagnostics to performing complicated medical procedures, or sending communications to flying starships. Depending on their technology, skills, and purpose, droids were grouped into five "classes,"''Star Wars: The Visual Encyclopedia''''Star Wars Super Graphic: A Visual Guide to a Galaxy Far, Far Away'' also called "degrees."

===First class droids===
The first class was made up of droids skilled in mathematical, physical, and medical sciences. Examples included the JN-66 analysis droid, the 2-1B surgical droid, the midwife droid, the DD-13 tripedal medical droid, and the GH-7 series medical assistance droid.

===Second class droids===
Droids of the second class were skilled in engineering and technical sciences. Examples included the DUM-series pit droid, the PLNK-series power droid, and the WED-15 Septoid Treadwell.

===Third class droids===
thumb|left|180px|B1-series battle droids were fourth class droids
Third class droids were skilled in social sciences and service functions. Examples included the CZ secretary droid, the LEP-series service droid, the FA-5 valet droid, and the BD-3000 luxury droid.

===Fourth class droids===
Droids of the fourth class were skilled in military and security functions. Examples included the GU-series Guardian police droid, the KX-series security droid, and the B1-series battle droid.

===Fifth class droids===
Fifth class droids were programmed for manual labor and intensive jobs that did not require a high level of intelligence. Examples included the GNK-series power droid, the MSE-6 series repair droid, the IW-37 pincer loader droid, the R-H029 sifter droid, the PK-series worker droid, and the UX-53 Autopolisher MK.II Droid.

==Behind the scenes==

The word ''droid'' was stylized as ''droid'' in the novelization of ''Star Wars: A New Hope'' and other early ''Star Wars'' Legends material.

In 1977, during an interview regarding the original trilogy film ''Star Wars'': Episode IV ''A New Hope'', George Lucas stated that droids were designed to serve sentients. However, droids were Lucas' favorite people, and the filmmaker did not want droids to be cold or malevolent, just like how the robots in ''THX 1138'' were "very friendly." On the other hand, he did not intend droids to be sentient themselves: he stated that, droids, even though some of them like C-3PO and R2-D2 are very human in nature, had no soul, only programmed to act like human beings.

Lucas wanted to focus on droids and their issues in life, giving them equal screentime since they had endured much suffering over the years and had little to no opportunities to prove themselves. As with Akira Kurosawa's film ''The Hidden Fortress'', which presents two peasants traveling with a princess and a general, ''A New Hope'' follows the points of view of two lowly beings&mdash;the droids R2-D2 and C-3PO.

===Trademark===
George Lucas holds a trademark on the term "droid." The miniature 'mech combat wargame ''BattleTech'' was originally released under the name ''BattleDroids'', but was renamed due to trademark issues with Lucasfilm.

Pixar Animation Studios' film ''The Incredibles'' (2004) contains a reference to Lucasfilm's droid with the large weapon of destruction, the Omnidroid; an apparently unrelated killer droid of the same name also appears in Star Wars Galaxies''. The credits of the film give a nod to Lucasfilm.

From ''Mickey News'' (25 November 2004):

:''[In] the closing credits... a notice that the term "Omnidroid" was used by permission of Lucasfilm Ltd. has prompted some fans to speculate that ''The Incredibles'' offers a sneak peak at a character from the next ''Star Wars'' film.''
:''"No. No, no, no," Bird [Brad Bird, director of ''The Incredibles''] says with a laugh, when asked if the secret weapon has a secret of its own. "I like that they think that, but it's more the term 'droid' is Lucas and we made the term Omnidroid (and then got the OK as a courtesy). So, no, there's no sneak. But God bless those fans. They're crazy."''

The term "Droid" has been licensed by Verizon Wireless for their line of smartphones based on the Android operating system. Motorola's late-2009 Google Android-based cell phone is called the Droid, and this line of phone has been expanded to include other Android-based phones released under Verizon. The term is used under license from Lucasfilm.
